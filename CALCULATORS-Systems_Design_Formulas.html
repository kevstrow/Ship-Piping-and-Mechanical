Ship Marine Systems Design Formulas

This page provides a comprehensive collection of formulas used in the design and analysis of shipboard marine systems. It includes equations for fluid flow, power calculations, volume, electrical systems, and unit conversions. Having all these key relationships in one place helps students quickly reference, compare, and apply essential engineering concepts when solving practical design problems or studying for exams.

Ship Tank Capacity Formulas

NOTE: Whenever calculating capacities, the applicable rules and regulations MUST be used. The formulas listed in the course notes may be superseded by the applicable rules and regulations.

Dirty Oil Tank Capacity

Dirty Oil Capacity = Lube Oil Storage Capacity × 1.05

  • Lube Oil Storage Capacity = total volume of stored clean oil
  • 1.05 = structural allowance (5%)

Oily Bilge Tank Capacity (General Rule)

Oily Bilge Capacity ≈ Dirty Oil Capacity × 1.5

  • Oily Bilge Capacity = volume of oily bilge tank
  • Dirty Oil Capacity = volume of dirty oil tank
  • 1.5 = general guideline for sizing (general rule of thumb is that the tank should be 50% larger than the Dirty Oil tank)

Note: Ships equipped with oily water separators may use smaller oily bilge tanks, as the separator treats and disposes of oily water onboard.

Dirty Oil Generation from Fuel Contamination

Dirty Oil per Day = (Fuel Tank Volume / 2000)

  • Fuel Tank Volume = total volume of fuel in gallons
  • 1 gallon of dirty oil is generated per 2000 gallons of fuel

This estimation is based on field data from ship marine engineers.

Day Tank Capacity

Capacity = Total Fuel Consumption × 8

  • Capacity = volume of day tank (liters or m³)
  • Total Fuel Consumption = rate of fuel use per hour

Minimum Fuel Capacity

Min Capacity = Fuel Rate × Mission Duration × 1.3 × 1.05

  • Fuel Rate = total fuel consumption per hour
  • Mission Duration = total expected time (hours)
  • 1.3 = fuel reserve safety factor
  • 1.05 = structural allowance (5%)

Fuel Overflow Tank Capacity

Overflow Capacity = Transfer Pump Rate × 10 minutes

  • Overflow Capacity = required overflow tank volume (liters or m³)
  • Transfer Pump Rate = flow rate of pump (liters or m³ per minute)

Conversion from Newtons per Square Meter to PSI

1 Pa = 0.000145038 PSI

PSI = Pa × 0.000145038

  • Pa = pressure in pascals (N/m²)
  • PSI = pressure in pounds per square inch

Potable Water Tank Capacity

Min Capacity = Number of Crew x Amount of Water Usage per Person x 1.05

  • Amount of Water Usage per Person = 68 liters/day
  • 1.05 = structural allowance (5%)

Black and Grey Water Tank Capacity

Black Water tank capacity = Potable Water capacity x 55%

Grey Water tank capacity = Potable Water capacity x 45%

Chain Locker Capacity

Volume of Chain Locker = 0.75 x (L x D²)

  • L = Length of chain in fathoms (1 fathom = 6 feet | 1 shot = 90 feet)
  • D = Diameter of chain in inches

Ship Range Calculations

Distance from Speed and Time

v = d / t

d = v × t

  • v = speed (knots)
  • d = distance (nautical miles)
  • t = time (hours)

Conversion to Kilometers

1 nautical mile = 1.852 kilometers

distancekm = distancenm × 1.852

  • distancekm = distance in kilometers
  • distancenm = distance in nautical miles

Ship Systems Calculations

NOTE: Whenever calculating system piping and component sizes, the applicable rules and regulations MUST be used. The formulas listed in the course notes may be superseded by the applicable rules and regulations.

Bilge Main Diameter

d = 1.68 x √(L x (B+D) + 25mm

  • d = internal diameter of bilge main, in millimeters
  • L = length between the ship’s perpendiculars, in meters
  • B = extreme breadth, in meters
  • D = moulded depth, in meters

Flow Rate for Pipes

Q = A × v

  • Q = flow rate (m³/s)
  • A = cross-sectional area of pipe (m²)
  • v = velocity of fluid (m/s)

Flow Rate for Tank Loading

Q = (Tank Volume / Time) * (1/60)

  • Q = flow rate (m³/s)
  • Tank Volume = Individual tank capacity (m³)
  • Time = Time to completely fill the tank (min)
  • 1/60 = Conversion rate m³/min to m³/s
  • NOTE: Flow rates near 2.1 m/s are typical. Do not exceed 4 m/s.

Fluid Velocity (v)

v = Q / A

  • v = fluid velocity (m/s)
  • Q = flow rate (m³/s)
  • A = pipe cross-sectional area (m²)

Pipe Diameter (D) 1

D = √(4Q / πv)

  • D = pipe inner diameter (m)
  • Q = flow rate (m³/s)
  • v = fluid velocity (m/s)

Pipe Diameter (D) 2

D = ( [(Q x 4) / (Velocity x 3.14)]^0.5 ) x 1000 mm)

  • Q = flow rate (m³/s)
  • v = fluid velocity (m/s)
  • 1000 mm = Conversion from meters to millimeters.

Pipe Cross Section Area (A)

A = (π x D²)

  • D = pipe inner diameter (m)

Pipe Slope

Slope = rise / run

  • Slope = dimensionless ratio
  • rise = vertical height change (m)
  • run = horizontal distance (m)

Continuity Equation (for varying diameters)

A₁ × v₁ = A₂ × v₂

  • A₁ = area of section 1 (m²)
  • v₁ = velocity at section 1 (m/s)
  • A₂ = area of section 2 (m²)
  • v₂ = velocity at section 2 (m/s)

Manning's Equation (gravity-driven pipe flow)

v = (1/n) × R2/3 × S1/2

  • v = velocity (m/s)
  • n = Manning's roughness coefficient
  • R = hydraulic radius = A / P (m)
  • S = slope of the pipe (dimensionless)

Friction Loss (Darcy-Weisbach)

hf = f × (L/D) × (v² / 2g)

  • hf = head loss due to friction (m)
  • f = friction factor (dimensionless)
  • L = length of pipe (m)
  • D = pipe diameter (m)
  • v = velocity (m/s)
  • g = gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²)

Energy and Power

Pump Efficiency

η = Pout / Pin

  • η = pump efficiency (decimal)
  • Pout = hydraulic power output (W)
  • Pin = input power to the pump (W)

Brake Horsepower (BHP)

BHP = (ρ × g × Q × H) / (3960 × η)

  • BHP = brake horsepower (HP)
  • ρ = fluid density (kg/m³)
  • g = gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²)
  • Q = flow rate (GPM)
  • H = head (m)
  • η = pump efficiency (decimal)
  • 3960 = constant (for HP from GPM and ft head)

Motor Horsepower (MHP)

MHP = BHP / motor efficiency

  • MHP = motor horsepower (HP)
  • BHP = brake horsepower (HP)
  • motor efficiency = decimal (e.g., 0.9)

Conversions

Flow Conversion: Cubic Meters per Second to GPM

1 m³/s = 15,850.3 US gallons per minute (GPM)

GPM = m³/s × 15,850.3

Horsepower to Kilowatts

1 HP = 0.7457 kW

kW = HP × 0.7457

Fluid Dynamics

Reynolds Number (Re)

Re = (ρ × v × D) / μ     or     Re = (v × D) / ν

  • Re = Reynolds number (dimensionless)
  • ρ = fluid density (kg/m³)
  • v = velocity (m/s)
  • D = pipe diameter (m)
  • μ = dynamic viscosity (Pa·s)
  • ν = kinematic viscosity (m²/s)

Volume Calculations

Volume of Cylinder

V = π × r² × h

  • V = volume (m³)
  • r = radius (m)
  • h = height or length (m)

Electrical Formulas

Ohm's Law

V = I × R

  • V = voltage (volts)
  • I = current (amperes)
  • R = resistance (ohms)

Electrical Power

P = V × I

  • P = power (watts)
  • V = voltage (volts)
  • I = current (amperes)

Three-Phase Power

P = √3 × V × I × pf

  • P = power (watts)
  • V = line voltage (volts)
  • I = line current (amperes)
  • pf = power factor (decimal)

Current (Amperage)

I = P / V

  • I = current (amperes)
  • P = power (watts)
  • V = voltage (volts)
